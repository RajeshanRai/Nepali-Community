Accessibility Statement

We are committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all community members, regardless of ability.

Last Updated: March 8, 2026

Our Commitment

The Nepali Community of Vancouver is dedicated to making our website, programs, and services accessible to everyone, including individuals with disabilities. We believe that accessibility is a fundamental right and strive to provide an inclusive experience for all community members.

We are committed to conforming with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA, as well as following best practices in web accessibility and inclusive design.

Accessibility Features

Our website and services incorporate numerous features to enhance accessibility:

Keyboard Navigation

Full keyboard accessibility for all interactive elements

Screen Reader Support

Optimized for JAWS, NVDA, and VoiceOver

Responsive Text

Text can be resized up to 200% without loss of functionality

Color Contrast

WCAG AA compliant color contrast ratios

Alt Text

Descriptive alternative text for all images

Media Captions

Captions and transcripts for video content

Website Accessibility Features

Navigation and Structure

  • Semantic HTML: Proper use of headings, landmarks, and ARIA labels
  • Skip Links: Quick navigation to main content areas
  • Consistent Layout: Predictable navigation and page structure
  • Breadcrumbs: Clear indication of page location within site hierarchy
  • Focus Indicators: Visible focus states for all interactive elements

Content and Readability

  • Clear Language: Plain language and simple sentence structures
  • Readable Fonts: Sans-serif fonts optimized for screen reading
  • Sufficient Spacing: Adequate line height and paragraph spacing
  • Responsive Design: Mobile-friendly layouts that adapt to different screen sizes
  • No Auto-Play: Media does not play automatically

Forms and Interactive Elements

  • Labeled Inputs: All form fields have descriptive labels
  • Error Messages: Clear, specific error messages with guidance for correction
  • Required Fields: Clear indication of required form fields
  • Time Limits: Sufficient time to complete forms, with options to extend
  • Confirmation Steps: Review and confirm before submitting important forms

Physical Event Accessibility

We strive to make our in-person events and programs accessible to all participants:

  • Venue Selection: Events held in wheelchair-accessible locations whenever possible
  • Accessibility Information: Event listings include detailed accessibility information
  • Transportation: Information about accessible parking and public transit options
  • Dietary Accommodations: Options for various dietary restrictions and allergies
  • Communication Support: Advance notice allows us to arrange interpreters or assistive services
  • Seating: Priority seating for individuals with mobility challenges
  • Service Animals: Service animals are welcome at all events
  • Sensory Considerations: Information about noise levels, lighting, and crowd sizes

Alternative Formats

We are committed to providing information in formats that work for everyone:

  • Documents: Available in HTML, PDF (accessible), and upon request in large print or Braille
  • Videos: Closed captions and transcripts provided
  • Audio: Transcripts available for audio-only content
  • Email Communications: Plain text options available
  • Printed Materials: Large print versions available upon request

If you need content in a specific format, please contact us at least 3 business days in advance, and we will do our best to accommodate your request.

Assistive Technologies

Our website is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen Readers: JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, TalkBack
  • Screen Magnification: ZoomText, MAGic
  • Speech Recognition: Dragon NaturallySpeaking
  • Alternative Input Devices: Switch controls, eye-tracking systems
  • Browser Accessibility Features: High contrast modes, text-to-speech

Ongoing Efforts

Accessibility is an ongoing journey, not a destination. We continually work to improve:

  • Regular Audits: Annual accessibility audits using automated and manual testing
  • User Testing: Feedback from users with disabilities guides our improvements
  • Staff Training: Team members receive accessibility training
  • Code Reviews: Accessibility checks integrated into development workflow
  • Third-Party Tools: Evaluation with WAVE, axe, Lighthouse, and other tools
  • Community Input: Active solicitation of feedback from community members

Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some limitations may exist:

  • Older PDF documents may not be fully accessible (we are working to remediate these)
  • Some third-party embedded content (maps, social media feeds) may have accessibility barriers
  • User-generated content may not always meet accessibility standards
  • Certain historical event photos may lack detailed alt text

We are actively working to address these limitations. If you encounter any accessibility barriers, please let us know so we can prioritize improvements.

Accommodations and Support

We want everyone to fully participate in our community. If you need accommodations to:

  • Access our website or digital content
  • Participate in events or programs
  • Communicate with our team
  • Volunteer or become a member

Please contact us as early as possible, and we will work with you to find suitable solutions. We do not charge fees for providing accessibility accommodations.

Feedback and Reporting

Your feedback helps us improve. If you:

  • Encounter an accessibility barrier on our website
  • Have difficulty accessing content or features
  • Have suggestions for improving accessibility
  • Would like to request an accommodation

Please contact our accessibility coordinator. We aim to respond to all accessibility feedback within 2 business days and resolve issues as quickly as possible.

Compliance and Standards

Our accessibility efforts align with:

  • WCAG 2.1 Level AA: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
  • AODA: Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (where applicable)
  • Section 508: U.S. Rehabilitation Act standards
  • EN 301 549: European accessibility standard
